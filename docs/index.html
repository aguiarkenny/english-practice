Recurring Errors to Watch

  • **Preposition "through" misused to translate "através de"** — appears when describing scope or distribution across groups; correct alternatives are "among," "across," or "within."
  • 1. **"for" + bare infinitive** — Student writes "for create" instead of "to create" or "for creating." This is a high-frequency Portuguese interference error (Portuguese uses "para criar" → incorrectly mapped to "for create").
  • 2. **False connector "Otherwise"** — Used to mean "on the other hand" (contrast), when it actually means "or else" (consequence). Likely influenced by Portuguese "caso contrário."
  • 3. **Noun adjunct pluralization** — "stakeholders interpretability" instead of "stakeholder interpretability." Student adds "-s" to modifying nouns, following Portuguese grammar patterns where adjective-noun agreement requires plural forms.
  • 1. **Subject-verb agreement with third-person singular:** Student omits the "-s" ending on present-tense verbs ("identify" instead of "identifies"). Monitor in future writing tasks.
  • 2. **Purpose clauses using "for + gerund" instead of "to + infinitive":** "For solving" instead of "To solve" — a direct Portuguese transfer pattern ("para resolver") that appears consistently.
  • 3. **Relative pronoun confusion ("what" vs. "which"):** Student uses "what" to introduce a clause referring to a preceding idea, where English requires "which." This is a recurring pattern among Portuguese speakers.
  • 1. **Dropped subject pronoun before a verb** — student omits "it" in clauses like "because allows" instead of "because it allows." This is a direct transfer from Portuguese, where subject pronouns are regularly omitted.
  • 2. **Incorrect use of "accordingly" instead of "according to"** — student confuses the adverb "accordingly" with the prepositional phrase "according to." Watch for similar adverb/preposition confusion in future writing.
  • 3. **Wrong verb form after auxiliary or complement structures** — e.g., "allows models handling" instead of "allows models to handle," and "while uses" instead of "while using." Student needs reinforcement of infinitive and gerund complement rules in English.
